Asian Salmon

Famous for their seafood, Asia's take on a beloved seafood dish serves to make your mouth water in eagerness. You will be excited at every bite you take and then some.

Served with soy, sesame sauce, and finely served hot-cooked rice, you will be persistent in your quest to devour this mouthwatering dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of salmon fillets, with skin
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of packed brown sugar
  • 2 cloves of minced garlic
  • 1 pinch of ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 2 cups of long-grain white rice
  • 1 teaspoon dried dill weed

    • Last, but not least

  • 3 cups of water

Steps

  1. Make several shallow slashes in the skinless side of the salmon fillets
  2. Place fillets skin-side down in a glass baking dish. In a medium bowl, whisk together the olive oil, rice vinegar, soy sauce, brown sugar. garlic, pepper, onion and sesame oil
  3. Pour the liquid over the salmon, cover, and refrigerate for 1 to 2 hours.
  4. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 Degrees C)
  5. In a medium saucepan, combine the rice, water and dill weed. Cover pan, bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and cook 20 minutes, until rice is tender and water has been absorbed
  6. Allow to stand for 5 minutes before removing lid and fluffing rice with a fork

    7. and Finally....

  7. Remove cover from the salmon, and bake the fish and then marinade for about 30 minutes, or until the fish can be flaked with a fork. Serve salmon over the rice, and pour the sauce over.
